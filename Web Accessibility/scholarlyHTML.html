1 What is the app in short? - Abstract

The Marvelous App aims to present a complex visualisation of the Marvel Comic Universe. Both people who don't know the comics and die-hard fans will get the chance to interact, search and find out information about the characters and the events tying them up. The app is a fun and interesting way to discover the Marvel characters, through means of Virtual Reality, Timelines and Interactive Design.

2 Why do we do it? - Motivation

The Marvel Universe is inherently complex. With a multitude of characters and actions spanning several years and continents, it is difficult to know where to start or who interacts with whom. The movies don't cover all of the information and reading all the comics takes time. For poeple who have never interacted with the universe, the task to get to know it seems daunting, and die-hard fans would like an outlet to share all their knowledge.

Our solution aims to bridge this gap, by offering an interactive and easy way to discover the universe. We propose several solutions, of different complexities, aimed at different types of users, to help them understand the relationships between the characters. It is also a pleasant way of following your passion and could help build the hype before a new movie release.

3 What is accessibility?

According to the Web Accessibility Initiative, web accessibility is the range at which all people, whatever their hardware, software, language, location, or ability are able to use the web. "When the Web meets this goal, it is accessible to people with a diverse range of hearing, movement, sight, and cognitive ability."

Thus, the aim of the web should be to make the barriers that people face in real life disappear, be they communication, interaction or motor barriers. The accessibility of a webite is measured in how well would people who live with cognitive, neurological, auditory, visual or other physical impairments interact, understand and work with a certain web application. Measuring web accessibility is not limited to people with disabilities, but to other categories as well, such as people accessing the application through different devices, such as smart phones, smart watches or other devices with small screens. It also applies to people with "temporary disabilities", such as a broken arm or lost glasses, to people with "situational limitations", such as bright sunlight or a loud area and even to people with slow internet connection or limited bandwidth.

In all of these cases, and not only, we want to be able to tell if users can efficiently use our applications.

4 What do we have here?

Home Screen - Presentation of main tabs and their roles, user options from home screen

The user is able to see the three main tabs that help organize the information: Events, Comics and Heroes. The information contained in each tab is self-explanatory. Once the user selects his/her option, the data regarding each tab is loaded and displayed in the screen below.

The user sees the information ordered chronologically, by year, with the most recent information on top. Clicking on a picture, he/she can also read more details about that specific item.

The user also has the option to scroll directly to the Bottom or back to the Top.

5 How do we measure accessibility?

The accessibility of an application concerns the degree to which it can be used effectively by people with various disabilities or impairments. It has to be taken into consideration initially, right from the design phase and checked constantly. While there are several tools one can use to measure and test the accessibility of an application, one should also use real-life user testing to have a more thourough and accurate idea.

In the following, we will present parts of the Accessibility Checklist and some of the tools we've used to test the Marvelious application.

Accessibility Checklist

The full accessibility checklist can be found here. It includes aspects like presenting alternatives - video, audio or images for your content, design aspects - such as the color pallete, the contrast and the text size, making functionalities available from the keyboard, help in navigating the web application and more.

A report on this checklist for our website is hereby presented in this work: although the app passed the Accessibility Checklist, it includes some suggestions on what to be careful about.

6 Where can we go from here? - Conclusion

We have established that the accessibility of a web application is important and should be tested properly, as the web should be accessible to everyone, everywhere. There are various tools that test out this aspect and we have used several of them.

Overall, the app is compliant with the accessibility standards, as shown by the results presented. There is, however, room for improvement, as the app could add further ways to present the information in a simpler way or in audio form or could add keyboard shortcuts, to name a few.
